# Takram Mini-Planet — Agent Handover

Date: 2026-07-16

## Current status

The Earth/local-surface examples work well enough for continued use and comparison:

- `/takram-clouds-spike`: direct Three.js/Takram baseline.
- `/takram-clouds`: Triangular Engine adapter version with controls, atmosphere, scene lighting, and cloud shadows.

The new `/takram-mini-planet` 100 km-radius experiment is **not working visually**. Do not treat custom-radius/global-view support as complete.

At planetary zoom, the 3D clouds are not recognisable as a coherent volumetric cloud layer. The current observed blocker is:

- animated concentric circles/bands, mainly blue shades, growing and shrinking near the planet centre;
- the bands can encase the entire planet as the camera zooms out;
- near the surface, clouds resemble separated curved pancakes/shell slices with large gaps rather than a continuous 3D volume;
- the clouds are mainly visible from the surface looking toward the underside of their spherical layer or near the limb, and are not visually useful from an exterior globe view;
- earlier configurations also produced jitter and black liquid/noise-like shapes that changed with camera perspective. `logarithmicDepthBuffer: true` removed much of the depth instability, but did not fix the concentric cloud/atmosphere bands.

## Intended result

1. Keep the working Takram near-surface experience.
2. Allow a configurable spherical planet radius; the current test radius is 100 km.
3. Fade in a conventional exterior 2D cloud texture at a chosen camera altitude.
4. Initially leave Takram itself unchanged during that fade. Do not add a hard whole-composer cutoff.
5. Eventually, if necessary, disable/fade only the volumetric cloud work at high altitude without disabling aerial perspective, tone mapping, or the whole composer.

## Adapter changes already made

- `<takram-atmosphere>` accepts `planetRadius` and `atmosphereHeight`.
- `TakramAtmosphereService.configurePlanet()` changes the shared spherical ellipsoid and `AtmosphereParameters.bottomRadius/topRadius`, regenerates LUTs, and derives the local-to-ECEF transform.
- `CloudsEffect` now receives the same shared `AtmosphereParameters` object as `AerialPerspectiveEffect`; it previously retained Takram's default Earth radii.
- Initial planet configuration now runs synchronously in `TakramAtmosphereComponent.ngOnInit()`. This matters because Takram snapshots radii into effect uniforms during effect construction.
- The generic `meshBasicMaterial` adapter accepts caller textures and `alphaMap`.

## Failed/discarded approaches

- Do not restrict orbit angles. The camera must remain able to view the planet from any angle; orbit controls were not the cause of earlier atmosphere distortion.
- Do not disable the entire post-processing composer at the texture transition altitude. That caused hard popping, removed atmosphere/tone mapping, and exposed a white renderer background.
- Do not use `local_weather.png` directly as the exterior globe texture. It does not represent Takram's final 3D density/lighting result and cannot visually match it.
- Do not claim that merely passing custom radii fixes custom planets. It fixed the gross Earth-sized atmosphere mismatch, but the 100 km result still has severe rendering artifacts.
- Keep local surface geometry at or above `y = 0`; below-surface geometry previously caused angle-dependent darkness, distortion, and black-hole-like globe views.

## Recommended next investigation

Make changes one at a time and ask for visual verification after each high-confidence change.

1. Add diagnostic toggles for **clouds**, **aerial perspective**, and the **2D shell** independently. The current controls do not isolate whether the blue concentric bands originate in `CloudsEffect`, `AerialPerspectiveEffect`, or their cloud overlay/shadow composition.
2. Reproduce the 100 km page with only the planet and aerial perspective, then add clouds. Compare screenshots at fixed camera positions/altitudes.
3. Test whether the rings are caused by the unusually thick relative atmosphere: 20 km is 20% of a 100 km radius. Try a 6 km atmosphere with a 2–4 km cloud layer, changing only this ratio.
4. Inspect Takram's Earth-tuned absolute parameters, not just radii: density profile scale heights, ray-march min/max step sizes, maximum ray distance, altitude correction, ellipsoid, and LUT sampling assumptions. Custom `bottomRadius/topRadius` support does not guarantee stable output at a radius ~64 times smaller than Earth.
5. Inspect cloud sampling specifically. The separated curved pancakes strongly suggest undersampling/ray-step banding or a coordinate-scale mismatch. Compare low/medium/high presets and expose the Takram cloud step-size/max-distance values temporarily for diagnosis.
6. Verify `worldToECEFMatrix`, ellipsoid, LUT radii, cloud atmosphere uniforms, and aerial atmosphere uniforms numerically after construction. They must all report the 100 km configuration, not Earth defaults.
7. Keep the 2D fade independent until the 3D defect is understood. The exterior texture is a fallback representation, not a fix for unstable volumetric rendering.

## Useful acceptance checks

- At the surface, cloud shapes look continuous and volumetric rather than like separated concentric shell slices.
- At fixed camera positions, the rendered structure does not grow/shrink or swim unless weather animation itself moves.
- Zooming to a full-globe view produces no concentric blue rings, black liquid/noise sphere, or perspective-dependent encasing shell.
- The exterior cloud texture remains visible beyond 1,000 km altitude and fades only according to the displayed opacity.
- Changing the planet radius keeps the mesh, orbit surface target, atmosphere, ellipsoid, cloud layer, LUTs, and camera clipping configuration mutually consistent.
